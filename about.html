I'm a computer engineering student at Colorado State University. I have an A.A.S. in electronics and a general associate of science degree from Front Range Community College. I've worked as an electronics technician, test-engineering technician, technical trainer, math and electrical engineering tutor. Recently, I've switched over to the software side of things and steered my path towards computer, rather than electrical engineering. I was finding more and more that building real life gizmos requires knowledge of code. After studying some basic code (C, C++, Java, python), it seemed more and more that a big part of computing is to connect to the rest of the world. Now I am studying web development. Making robots work through internet control would be living the dream.
